EWU Small Business Association CyberSecurity Knowledge Base

EWU's guide to protecting small businesses from cyber threats.

Compliance and Regulation

Compliance and regulation is a broad topic refering to and laws, standards, and guidelines that businesses are compelled to follow. These regulations are designed to protect businesses, consumers, and the relating infrastructure and data. These requirements vary drastically depending on the location of the business, the type of business that is run, and the data that is stored and processed.

Data Protection Regulations

Regulatory bodies protect end users by ensuring that consumer data is handled responsibly and securely. The major regulator bodies in America include the Federal Trade Commission (FTC), the Department of Health and Human Services (HHS), the Securities and Exchange Commission (SEC), and the Payment Card Industry (PCI). Businesses may also be subject to state level regulations such as the California Consumer Privacy Act (CCPA). Any businesses operating internationally may also find themselves subject to the General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR). These should serve as the backbone of any business' compliance and regulation strategy, which should, in turn, inform good policy.

Federal Trade Commission

The FTC is a federal agency that enforces antitrust and consumer protection laws. The FTC approaches this via law enforcement, advocacy, research, and education. Their website contains several resources for businesses to help protect themselves and their customers. These include privacy and security regulations, a helpful industry selector, a small business help guide, and several links to plain language guidance to help business get up and running. These are but a small sample of resources available to businesses on the FTC website.

Department of Health and Human Services

The HHS is a federal agency that is responsible for protecting the consumer health data of Americans. Their website is an excellent resource for laws and regulations. This page details regulations such as the Health Insurance Portability and Accountability Act (HIPPA), the Health Information Technology for Economic and Clinical Health (HITECH), and many other regulatory acts.

Securities and Exchange Commission

The SEC is a federal agency that enforces federal securities laws. Their website contains a number of resources dedicated to protecting businesses, investors, and the markets. Of particular interest to cyber security is their page dedicated to cyber security threats such as ransomware and other cyber related crimes.

Industry Standards

Industry standards are a set of guidelines that are designed to help businesses protect themselves from cyber threats. These standards are designed to be flexible and scalable, meeting the demands of businesses and their clients. The major industry standards include the National Institute of Standards and Technology (NIST), the Center for Internet Security (CIS), the Payment Card Industry (PCI).

National Institute of Standards and Technology

NIST is a non-regulatory federal agency that is responsible for developing technology, metrics, and standards to drive innovation and economic competitiveness in US-based businesses. Their cybersecurity framework is comprised of a set of standards, guidelines, and best practices to manage cybersecurity-related risk. Their website gives a comprehensive overview of their focus areas and assignments.

Center for Internet Security

The CIS is a non-profit entity that is responsible for developing best practices for cyber defense. Their website offers a number of resources for businesses to help protect themselves from cyber threats. These include a number of tools to help businesses get started and a best practices guide.

Payment Card Industry

The PCI is a non-federal entity that is responsible for protecting and managing consumer payments made with payment cards. They are responsible for managing the PCI Data Security Standard (PCI DSS) which is a set of security standards designed to ensure that all companies that accept, process, store, or transmit credit card information maintain a secure environment. Their website offers training, resources and a generalized set of standards.
