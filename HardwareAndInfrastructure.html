Hardware and Infrastructure

This section will broadly cover the physical and digital components of security. Physical and digital security are intrinsically linked, as physical breaches can lead to digital breaches and vice versa. It is important to look at both aspects of security to ensure that your business is protected. Policy will play a key role in the development of security plans.

Physical Security

Physical security is the means by which you protect your assets, to include hardware, networks, data, and personnel from physical actions. The most basic of these are deterrents such as locks, fences, cameras, and security personnel. The extent of these measures will largely depend on the needs of the business. For computers that have sensitive data, a business might consider placing these in a secure room with the addition of locking cases to prevent physical theft of the drives.

Endpoint Security

Endpoints refer to any devices that connect to a network. This is commonly computers, laptops, tablets, and smartphones. Endpoint security is the means by which these devices are protected. Endpoint security to include antivirus software, firewalls, and encryption. Consistent updates are key to maintaining the security of these devices.

Firewalls

Firewalls are a key component of network security. These serve as a barrier between your local network and the internet. Firewalls can implemented at the hardware or software levels. Hardware firewalls are physical devices that are placed between the network and the internet. Software firewalls are configurable programs installed on computers, routers, and servers. Firewalls can be configured to block or allow traffic based on a set of rules. They can also be configured to log traffic and alert administrators to potential threats.

Routers and Switches

Routers and switches are common physical components of a network. Routers are responsible for directing traffic between networks, while switches are responsible for directing traffic within a network. These both can be configured to block or allow traffic based on a rule set that fits required business needs.

In the case of wifi enabled routers, it is important to have a secure password enabled to prevent unauthorized access. Business's should assess the need for guest wifi networks and the security implications of these networks, as they can be a potential vector for attack.

Server Security

Servers can server many functions such as hosting websites, storing data, and running applications. To secure servers it is important to make sure they are up to date, are protected with strong passwords, and are stored in secure locations. Companies should also strive to limit access to servers to only those who need it.

Internet of Things Security

The Internet of Things (IoT) usually refers to any device connected to a network that is not a computer or smartphone. These devices can be printers, lights, thermostats, and much more. These devices can be vulnerable to attack if not properly secured. It is important to make sure that these devices are updated, have strong passwords, and are not accessible to unauthorized users.
